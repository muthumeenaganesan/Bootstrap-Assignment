The new Ford Escape

Ford's best-selling SUV gets a mid-cycle update for the 2017 model year, adding new technologies, two new engines, a start-stop function, and a new look inside and out.

New front- and rear-end styling are part of the revamp, but the more important changes come under the hood. The base 2.5-liter 4-cylinder engine is carried over, but mid-range Escapes now offer a 1.5-liter turbocharged inline-4 (in place of a 1.6-liter unit) or a new 2.0-liter turbocharged 4-cylinder. Power output is about 179 horsepower for the smaller turbocharged inline-4, and 245 hp for the 2.0-liter version. Fuel economy should see some improvement, since both will come with start-stop technology, and handling should improve with new springs and shocks.

The interior was only slightly changed, with some new features such as fast-charging USB ports, updated climate and steering-wheel controls, and an electronic parking brakeâ€”but Ford says the addition of more soundproofing makes the Escape a quieter place to be.

New safety equipment will include forward-collision warnings with automatic emergency braking, parking assistance, and lane-departure and lane-keeping assistance. However, the Escape's styling changes may not be enough to improve its "Poor" rating on the IIHS latest small overlap front crash test.
